## 1. Kettle-Only "Cooking Hacks" for Data Analysts
Since you have no stove and are cooking in a shared PG with only two electric kettles, you must protect the heating elements from burning while ensuring your food is thoroughly cooked. 

### The "Soaking Method" for Poha and Suji (No Kettle Contact)
Never put grains directly inside the kettle. Grains will stick to the heating coil, burn, and destroy the appliance. Instead, use the **Steam-Soaking Method** in a heat-safe bowl:

1. **Instant Steam-Cooked Poha:**
   * **Prep:** Measure 50g–60g of Poha in a bowl. Rinse it twice with tap water to clean it and wash away excess starch, then drain the water completely.
   * **Boil:** Boil fresh water in your designated water kettle.
   * **Steam:** Pour exactly 3–4 tablespoons of boiling water over the damp Poha. Add a pinch of salt, turmeric, and Maggi Masala.
   * **Cover & Trapped Steam:** Immediately cover the bowl tightly with a plate. Let it sit for **5–7 minutes**. The trapped residual steam will swell and cook the Poha perfectly without making it mushy or stick to any elements. Top with roasted peanuts and fresh lemon juice.

2. **Kettle-Steam Suji Upma:**
   * **Prep:** In a bowl, mix 40g of Suji (Semolina), 10g of raw peanuts, salt, turmeric, red chilli powder, and 1 teaspoon of ghee (ghee lubricates the grains and prevents clumping).
   * **Ratio:** Pour boiling water into the bowl in a **1:2 ratio** (approx. 80ml water for 40g Suji).
   * **Stir & Cover:** Stir quickly and vigorously with a spoon to prevent any lumps. Cover tightly with a plate and let it rest for **8–10 minutes**. The Suji will fully hydrate and cook in the trapped heat, resulting in a fluffy, warm upma.

### Perfect Kettle-Boiled Eggs (Zero Cracks)
* **Rule 1:** Use your second kettle (the cooking kettle), never your drinking water kettle.
* **Rule 2:** Avoid thermal shock. If you store eggs in a fridge, bring them to room temperature before boiling. Placing cold eggs in hot water causes the air pocket inside to expand rapidly, cracking the shell.
* **Step-by-Step:**
  1. Place a small, clean washcloth or a silicone mesh at the bottom of the kettle (this buffers the eggs from the direct heat of the metal bottom).
  2. Place **2 eggs** gently on the cloth. Submerge them in cold water by at least 1 inch.
  3. Turn the kettle on. Once the water reaches a rolling boil and the kettle automatically switches off, **do not turn it back on**.
  4. Leave the eggs in the hot water for **12–14 minutes**. The residual heat will cook them to a perfect hard-boiled state with creamy yolks and no green sulfur ring.
  5. Plunge them into cold water, peel, and eat.

### How to "Bulk" Grains with Cheap Ludhiana Veggies
Vegetables add essential fiber, vitamins, and volume to keep you full without adding heavy calories. Since you cannot sauté veggies, use the **Steep-Blanching Method**:
* **The Veggies:** Buy cabbage and onions. They are cheap, stay fresh for a long time without refrigeration, and add a satisfying crunch.
* **The Method:** Finely shred 100g of cabbage and 50g of onions. Place them in your bowl. Pour rolling boiling water over them and cover for **3 minutes**. 
* **The Mix:** Drain the water. The veggies will be slightly softened (blanched) but will retain their crunch and nutrients. Mix them directly into your soaked Poha or Suji. This increases your meal volume by 150g for just ₹8!

### Avoiding Mid-Afternoon Brain Fog during Data Projects
Data analysis requires high focus. Mid-afternoon brain fog (typically 3:00 PM – 4:00 PM) is usually caused by an **insulin spike and subsequent crash** from eating high-carbohydrate lunches (such as simple white bread, instant noodles, or sweet biscuits) or simple depletion of glucose.

* **Lunch Rule:** Add cabbage and onions to your Suji or Poha. The fiber slows down digestion, ensuring glucose enters your bloodstream gradually. Avoid sugary tea or biscuits with lunch.
* **The Cognitive Window (3:30 PM):** Eat **20g of roasted peanuts** and **20g of roasted makhana** at this time. 
  * *Why Peanuts?* Peanuts contain healthy fats and L-arginine, which helps dilate blood vessels, improving blood flow and oxygen delivery to the brain.
  * *Why Makhana?* Makhana is a low-glycemic index food. It provides slow-release carbohydrates that replenish liver glycogen without triggering an insulin surge, maintaining focus through your final analysis tasks.

## 3. Ludhiana Survival & Shopping Guide
Ludhiana is a vibrant industrial city, but as a solo woman living there for the first time, navigating the local market is crucial for staying within your budget.

### Smart Shopping Guidelines
1. **Official Dairy (Verka Booths):**
   * *The Hack:* Do not buy milk from local open dairies (doodh walas) or random stores. They often dilute milk or inflate prices. 
   * *Action:* Buy exclusively from official **Verka Booths** (Punjab’s state cooperative). Prices are fixed by the government, printed on the bag, and the quality is guaranteed. A 500ml packet of Verka standard milk is around ₹28–₹30.
2. **Bulk Neighborhood Kirana Stores:**
   * *The Hack:* Avoid modern grocery stores or supermarkets (like reliance or DMart) for loose items, as they tempt you to spend on packaged goods.
   * *Action:* Walk into a local neighborhood wholesale Kirana shop. Buy Poha, Suji, and Peanuts in loose, unbranded packets (1kg or 2kg bags). Branded, packaged oats cost ₹160/kg, while loose oats/grains at a local Kirana cost ₹60–₹80/kg.
3. **Evening Vegetable Market (Sabzi Mandi):**
   * *The Hack:* Vegetable vendors in local Ludhiana Rehri markets charge premium prices in the morning.
   * *Action:* Shop between **7:30 PM and 8:30 PM**. Vendors want to clear out their perishable inventory before closing and will sell cabbage, onions, tomatoes, and potatoes at discounts of up to 40%.

### Egg Price Rip-Off Detector
* **NECC Wholesale Rate:** The National Egg Coordination Committee (NECC) sets standard daily egg prices in Ludhiana. The benchmark rate is approximately **₹155 per crate of 30 eggs** (approx. **₹5.19 per egg**).
* **Spotting Overcharging:** 
  * Local shopkeepers often charge a retail premium. A price of **₹6.00 per egg** (or ₹36 for half a dozen) is a fair retail markup.
  * If a shopkeeper charges you **₹7.00 to ₹8.00 per egg**, you are being overcharged by 35% to 54%. 
  * *Mentor Advice:* To combat this, ask for a "half-crate" (15 eggs) at a larger local shop or Verka Booth, which usually retail closer to the wholesale rate.
